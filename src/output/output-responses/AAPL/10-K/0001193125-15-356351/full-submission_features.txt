 Apple Inc. is a multinational technology company that designs, develops, and sells consumer electronics products such as smartphones, tablets, and computers. It was founded by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne in 1976. Apple is currently headquartered in Cupertino, California, and is listed on the NASDAQ Global Select Market under the ticker symbol "AAPL".

According to the information provided in the 2015 Form 10-K/A Annual Report, as of September 26, 2015, Apple had $74.1 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments. Apple generated net income for the fiscal year ending September 26, 2015 of $33.3 billion. The Company's largest revenue segment is Sales of Products for $79.6 billion, followed by Services sales at $11.4 billion.

Apple's balance sheet shows assets of $123.2 billion, liabilities of $58.8 billion, and stockholders' equity of $64.4 billion. The Company's current ratio is 1.38 to 1, and the debt-to-equity ratio is 0.55 to 1.

In terms of its operating environment, Apple operates in the high technology industry, with a focus on computer and mobile devices. The company has a strong brand and a loyal customer base, which gives it an advantage over its competitors.

Overall, the information provided in the 2015 Form 10-K/A shows that Apple is a financially stable and successful company with a strong position in the high technology industry.
