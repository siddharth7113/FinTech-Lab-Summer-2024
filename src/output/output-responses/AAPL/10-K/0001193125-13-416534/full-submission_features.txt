 This document is the Form 10-K financial report for Apple, Inc. for the fiscal year ended September 28, 2013. It contains audited financial statements, as well as a discussion of the company's operations and financial position.

The net income for the fiscal year was $ 37.4 billion, which was an increase from $ 27.4 billion in the previous year. Net sales were $ 157.7 billion, also an increase from $ 101.2 billion the previous year. The company's gross profit margin was 41.7 percent.

In terms of liquidity, the company had $ 43.4 billion in cash and cash equivalents at the end of the year, an increase from $ 18.0 billion the previous year. It also paid $ 10.6 billion in dividends, an increase of $ 1.5 billion from the previous year.

Under the "Risk Factors" section, the company disclosed various risks that could negatively impact its business, including market risk, operational risk, and credit risk. It also discussed potential changes in technology competition, privacy regulations, and political and economic uncertainty.

Finally, the Sarbanes-Oxley Report of Independent Directors was included in the form, which stated that the directors acted solely in the best interests of the company and its shareholders. The report also discussed the directors' experience in the industry and their independence from management.
